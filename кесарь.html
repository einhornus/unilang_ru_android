ке́сарь (23416)


Etymology

Borrowed from Old Church Slavonic ке́сарь (kesarĭ), from Koine Greek Καῖσαρ (Kaîsar), itself a borrowing from Latin Caesar. Doublet of царь (carʹ), це́зарь (cézarʹ), and це́сарь (césarʹ).

Noun

ке́сарь • (késarʹ) m anim (genitive ке́саря, nominative plural ке́сари, genitive plural ке́сарей)

  1. (historical, archaic) Caesar (title of a Roman or Byzantine emperor)
    ке́сарю ке́сарево ― késarju késarevo ― render unto Caesar (literally, “unto Caesar [the things that are] Caesar’s”)

Synonyms

  • це́зарь m (cézarʹ); це́сарь m (césarʹ) (archaic)

Derived terms

  • ке́сарев (késarev)
