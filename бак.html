бак (4228)


Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Dutch bakboord or German Backbord.

Noun[edit]

бак • (bak) | m | inan (genitive ба́ка, nominative plural ба́ки, genitive plural ба́ков)

  1. (nautical) forecastle

Etymology 2[edit]

Borrowed from Dutch bak or German Back or English back

Noun[edit]

бак • (bak) | m | inan (genitive ба́ка, nominative plural ба́ки, genitive plural ба́ков)

  1. container, receptacle (for liquids)
  2. tank
    то́пливный бак ― tóplivnyj bak ― fuel tank
    бензи́новый бак / бензоба́к ― benzínovyj bak / benzobák ― petrol tank
  3. boiler
Related terms[edit]
  • бачо́к (bačók)

Etymology 3[edit]

Contraction of бакенба́рда (bakenbárda).

Noun[edit]

бак • (bak) | m | inan (genitive ба́ка, nominative plural ба́ки, genitive plural ба́ков)

  1. sideburn
    Synonym: бакенба́рда (bakenbárda)
