<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">book</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">books</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>A collection of sheets of paper bound together to hinge at one edge, containing printed or written material, pictures, etc.
<ul><li><div class="wiktQuote" data-validation="yellow"><b>1610 – 1611</b>, William Shakespeare, <i>The Tempest</i> 1.2:<dl><dd><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">Knowing I loved my <b>books</b>, he furnished me / From mine own library with volumes that / I prize above my dukedom.</span></dd></dl></div></li>
<li><b>1962</b>, James East Irby translating <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luis_Borges" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Luis Borges">Luis Borges</a> as "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Library_of_Babel" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Library of Babel">The Library of Babel</a>":
<dl><dd>I repeat: it suffices that a <b>book</b> be possible for it to exist. Only the impossible is excluded. For example: no <b>book</b> can be a ladder, although no doubt there are <b>books</b> which discuss and negate and demonstrate this possibility and others whose structure corresponds to that of a ladder.</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>1983</b>, Steve Horelick amp; al., "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reading_Rainbow" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Reading Rainbow">Reading Rainbow</a>":
<dl><dd>I can be anything.<br />Take a look!<br />It's in a <b>book</b>:<br />A reading rainbow.</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>1991</b>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Fry" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Stephen Fry">Stephen Fry</a>, <i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Liar_(novel)" class="extiw" title="w:The Liar (novel)">The Liar</a></i>, page 51:
<dl><dd>Trefusis's quarters could be described in one word. <b>Books</b>. <b>Books</b> and <b>books</b> and <b>books</b>. And then, just when an observer might be lured into thinking that that must be it, more <b>books</b>... Trefusis himself was highly dismissive of them. ‘Waste of trees,’ he had once said. ‘Stupid, ugly, clumsy, heavy things. The sooner technology comes up with a reliable alternative the better... The world is so fond of saying that <b>books</b> should be “treated with respect”. But when are we told that <i>words</i> should be treated with respect?’</dd></dl></li></ul>
<dl><dd><i>She opened the <b>book</b> to page 37 and began to read aloud.</i></dd>
<dd><i>He was frustrated because he couldn't find anything about dinosaurs in the <b>book</b>.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li>A long work fit for publication, typically prose, such as a novel or textbook, and typically published as such a bound collection of sheets, but now sometimes electronically as an e-book.
<dl><dd><i>I have three copies of his first <b>book</b>.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li>A major division of a long work.
<dl><dd><i>Genesis is the first <b>book</b> of the Bible.</i></dd>
<dd><i>Many readers find the first <b>book</b> of </i>A Tale of Two Cities<i> to be confusing.</i></dd>
<dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonyms:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">tome</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">volume</span></span></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">gambling</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A record of betting (from the use of a notebook to record what each person has bet).
<dl><dd><i>I'm running a <b>book</b> on who is going to win the race.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li>A convenient collection, in a form resembling a book, of small paper items for individual use.
<dl><dd><i>a <b>book</b> of stamps</i></dd>
<dd><i>a <b>book</b> of raffle tickets</i></dd>
<dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonym:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">booklet</span></span></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">theater</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The script of a musical or opera.
<dl><dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonym:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">libretto</span></span></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">usually in the plural</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Records of the accounts of a business.
<dl><dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonyms:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">account</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">record</span></span></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">law<span class="ib-comma">,</span> colloquial</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A book award, a recognition for receiving the highest grade in a class (traditionally an actual book, but recently more likely a letter or certificate acknowledging the achievement).</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">whist</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Six tricks taken by one side.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">poker slang</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> four of a kind<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="cite_note-3"> | 3 | </a></sup></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">sports</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A document, held by the referee, of the incidents happened in the game.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">sports<span class="ib-comma">,</span> by extension</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A list of all players who have been booked (received a warning) in a game.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>2011</b> March 2,  Andy Campbell,  “Celtic 1 - 0 Rangers”, in  <cite>BBC</cite> | <sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/9409758.stm">[1]</a></sup>:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">Celtic captain Scott Brown joined team-mate Majstorovic in the <b>book</b> and Rangers' John Fleck was also shown a yellow card as an ill-tempered half drew to a close.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">cartomancy</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The twenty-sixth Lenormand card.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">figuratively</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Any source of instruction.</li></ol>
