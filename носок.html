носо́к (2989)


Etymology 1

Likely from нос (nos, “nose”) + | -о́к (-ók); compare etymology 2. Sense development is "little nose" -gt; "projection" -gt; "toe" -gt; "sock". Less likely from носи́ть (nosítʹ, “to wear”) + | -о́к (-ók).

Noun

носо́к • (nosók) m inan (genitive носка́, nominative plural носки́, genitive plural носко́в)

  1. sock
    надева́ть носки́ ― nadevátʹ noskí ― to put on socks
  2. toe (of a boot, shoe or sock)
  3. toe (of a foot or hoof; especially the tip of the toe)
    приподнима́ться на носки́ ― pripodnimátʹsja na noskí ― to rise on one's toes
  4. projection, nose (e.g. of a plane)

Etymology 2

нос (nos) + | -о́к (-ók)

Noun

носо́к • (nosók) m inan (genitive *носка́, nominative plural *носки́, genitive plural *носко́в)

  1. Diminutive of нос (nos, “nose”)
Synonyms
  • но́сик (nósik) (all cases exist)

Etymology 3

Noun

но́сок • (nósok) f inan pl

  1. genitive plural of но́ска (nóska)

Etymology 4

Adjective

но́сок • (nósok)

  1. short masculine singular of но́ский (nóskij)
