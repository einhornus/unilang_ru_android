тулу́п (9487)


Etymology[edit]

Possibly from Turkic or some other Altaic language. The exact origin and its jaunt, per Vasmer, is hard to ascertain given that the borrowing is found in other Slavic languages such as Ukrainian тулуб (túlub), Belarusian тулу́п (túlup). A more distant cognate is Turkish tulum.

Noun[edit]

тулу́п • (tulúp) | m | inan (genitive тулу́па, nominative plural тулу́пы, genitive plural тулу́пов)

  1. sheepskin coat, overcoat, tulup (Russian-style long warm sheepskin/hareskin coat with a collar)
