тулу́п (9489)


Etymology

Possibly from Turkic or some other Altaic language. The exact origin and its jaunt, per Vasmer, is hard to ascertain given that the borrowing is found in other Slavic languages such as Ukrainian ту́луб (túlub), Belarusian ту́луп (túlup). A more distant cognate is Turkish tulum.

Noun

тулу́п • (tulúp) m inan (genitive тулу́па, nominative plural тулу́пы, genitive plural тулу́пов)

  1. sheepskin coat, overcoat, tulup (Russian-style long warm sheepskin/hareskin coat with a collar)
