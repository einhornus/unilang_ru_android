кла́няться (5959)


Etymology

From dialectal кла́нять (klánjatʹ, “to make bow”) + | -ся (-sja), inherited from Proto-Slavic *klanjati. Iterative of клони́ть (klonítʹ), with unexpected stress shift and root vowel lengthening.

Verb

кла́няться • (klánjatʹsja) impf (perfective поклони́ться)

  1. to bow (to, before), to greet (to bend oneself as a gesture of respect or deference)
  2. to give/send regards to someone
    кла́няйтесь ему́ от меня́.
    Klánjajtesʹ jemú ot menjá.
    Give him my regards.
  3. to cringe (before); to humiliate oneself (before); to humbly beg

Related terms

  • покло́н (poklón)
