ено́т (19498)


Etymology[edit]

From German Genette, from French genette, with unclear -о-. The original meaning was “genet”.

Noun[edit]

ено́т • (jenót) | m | anim (genitive ено́та, nominative plural ено́ты, genitive plural ено́тов)

  1. (obsolete) genet
  2. raccoon (mammal)
  3. raccoon fur, coonskin

Related terms[edit]

  • енотовый (jenótovyj)
  • енотовидный (jenotovídnyj)
  • енотиха (jenótixa)
