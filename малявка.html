маля́вка (31110)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *maljavъka (compare with Belarusian маляўка (maljaŭka), Polish dialectal malawka, Ukrainian маля́вка (maljavka)), from *maljava + | *-ъka whence Russian dialectal маля́ва (maljáva, “fry”) (also, compare with Czech malavý, Macedonian малав (malav), Serbo-Croatian malava), further from *malъ (“small; little”) + | *-ava, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(s)mel-.

Noun

маля́вка • (maljávka) f anim (genitive маля́вки, nominative plural маля́вки, genitive plural маля́вок)

  1. (colloquial) whitebait
  2. (colloquial) little one (a young child); shorty, shrimp (a short person)

Synonyms

  • (whitebait): малёк (maljók)

Derived terms

  • маля́вочка (maljávočka)

Related terms

  • ма́лый (mályj)
