кабала́ (28441)


Etymology 1[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic кабала́ (kabala, “document, deed, debt issue”), from Turkish kabal (“certain quantity, tasked labour, one day labour”).

Noun[edit]

кабала́ • (kabalá) | f | inan (genitive кабалы́, nominative plural кабалы́, genitive plural каба́л)

  1. (historical) promissory note, leading to debt bondage if unpaid
  2. (historical) debt bondage
  3. enslavement, servitude, bondage

Etymology 2[edit]

Borrowed from Hebrew קַבָּלָה‎ | (“receiving/tradition”).

Noun[edit]

кабала́ • (kabalá) | f | inan (genitive кабалы́, uncountable)

  1. (religion) kabbalah
Synonyms[edit]
  • каббала (kabbalá)
Related terms[edit]
  • кабалистика (kabalístika), каббалистика (kabbalístika)
