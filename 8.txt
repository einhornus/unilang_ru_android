<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English" class="extiw" title="w:Middle English">Middle English</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">accion</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French" class="extiw" title="w:Old French">Old French</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro">action</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">āctiō</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">act of doing or making</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, from <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">āctus</i>, perfect passive participle of <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">agō</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">do, act</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, + action suffix <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">-iō</i>; see <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">act</i>.
</p>
<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">action</strong> (<i>countable and uncountable</i>, <i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">actions</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Something done so as to accomplish a purpose.</li>
<li>A way of motion or functioning.
<dl><dd><i>Knead bread with a rocking <b>action</b>.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li>Fast-paced activity.
<dl><dd><i>a movie full of exciting <b>action</b></i></dd></dl></li>
<li>A mechanism; a moving part or assembly.
<dl><dd><i>a rifle <b>action</b></i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">music</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The mechanism, that is the set of moving mechanical parts, of a keyboard instrument, like a piano, which transfers the motion of the key to the sound-making device.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="cite_note-1"> | 1 | </a></sup></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">music</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The distance separating the strings and the fretboard on a guitar.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">slang</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Sexual intercourse.
<dl><dd><i>She gave him some <b>action</b>.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">military</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Combat.
<dl><dd><i>He saw some <b>action</b> in the Korean War.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">law</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A charge or other process in a law court (also called <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">lawsuit</i> and <i>actio</i>).</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">mathematics</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A mapping from a pairing of mathematical objects to one of them, respecting their individual structures. The pairing is typically a Cartesian product or a tensor product. The object that is not part of the output is said to <i>act</i> on the other object. In any given context, <i>action</i> is used as an abbreviation for a more fully named notion, like group action or <i>left</i> group action.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">physics</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The product of energy and time, especially the product of the Lagrangian and time.</li>
<li>The event or connected series of events, either real or imaginary, forming the subject of a play, poem, or other composition; the unfolding of the drama of events.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">art<span class="ib-comma">,</span> painting and sculpture</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The attitude or position of the several parts of the body as expressive of the sentiment or passion depicted.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">bowling</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> spin put on the bowling ball.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">business<span class="ib-comma">,</span> obsolete<span class="ib-comma">,</span> a Gallicism</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A share in the capital stock of a joint-stock company, or in the public funds.
<ul><li><span class="maintenance-line" style="color: 777777;">(Can we date this quote by Burke and provide title, author’s full name, and other details?)</span>
<dl><dd>the Euripus of funds and <b>actions</b></dd></dl></li></ul></li></ol>
