<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">hum</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">hums</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>A hummed tune, i.e. created orally with lips closed.</li>
<li>An often indistinct sound resembling human humming.
<dl><dd><i>They could hear a <b>hum</b> coming from the kitchen, and found the dishwasher on.</i></dd></dl>
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><i><abbr title="circa">c.</abbr></i> <b>1606</b>,  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare" class="extiw" title="w:William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>,  “<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macbeth" class="extiw" title="w:Macbeth">The Tragedie of Macbeth</a>”, in  <cite>Mr. William Shakespeares Comedies, Histories, amp; Tragedies: Published According to the True Originall Copies</cite> (<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Folio" class="extiw" title="w:First Folio">First Folio</a>), London |  Printed by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Jaggard" class="extiw" title="w:William Jaggard">Isaac Iaggard</a>, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Blount" class="extiw" title="w:Edward Blount">Ed[ward] Blount</a>, published 1623, <small><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OCLC" class="extiw" title="w:OCLC">OCLC</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://worldcat.org/oclc/606515358">606515358</a></small>, [Act III, scene ii]:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">the shard-borne beetle with his drowsy <b>hums</b></span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li>Busy activity, like the buzz of a beehive.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Britain" class="extiw" title="w:Great Britain">Britain</a><span class="ib-comma">,</span> slang</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> unpleasant odour.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">dated</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> An imposition or hoax; humbug.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">obsolete</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A kind of strong drink.
