<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">stun</strong> (<i>third-person singular simple present</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en 3 | s | pres-form-of" lang="en">stuns</b>, <i>present participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en pres | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">stunning</b>, <i>simple past and past participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en past | and | past | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">stunned</b>)
</p>
<ol><li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">transitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To incapacitate; especially by inducing disorientation or unconsciousness.
<dl><dd><i>Bill tried to <b>stun</b> the snake by striking it on the head.</i></dd>
<dd><i>In many European countries cattle have to be <b>stunned</b> before slaughtering.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">transitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To shock or surprise.
<dl><dd><i>The celebrity was <b>stunned</b> to find herself confronted with unfounded allegiations on the front page of a newspaper.</i></dd>
<dd><i>He stood there <b>stunned</b>, looking at the beautiful, breath-taking sunrise.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">snooker<span class="ib-comma">,</span> billiards</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To hit the cue ball so that it slides without topspin or backspin (and with or without sidespin) and continues at a natural angle after contact with the object ball</li></ol>
