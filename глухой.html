глухо́й (19316)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *gluxъ.

Adjective[edit]

глухо́й • (gluxój) (comparative (по)глуше)

  1. deaf
  2. dull, vague, muffled (sound)
  3. (phonology) unvoiced, voiceless (consonant)
    Antonym: зво́нкий (zvónkij)
  4. out-of-the-way, remote, god-forsaken (place)
  5. overgrown, wild (forest)
  6. dead-end

Noun[edit]

глухо́й • (gluxój) | m | anim (genitive глухо́го, nominative plural глухи́е, genitive plural глухи́х, feminine глуха́я)

  1. deaf person
  2. (plural) the deaf

Related terms[edit]

  • гло́хнуть (glóxnutʹ)
