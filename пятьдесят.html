пятьдеся́т (1202)


Etymology

Compounded from пять + де́сять, or five tens. Десят was the Old East Slavic genitive plural, which is required by numbers пять and up.

Numeral

пятьдеся́т • (pjatʹdesját)

  1. fifty (50)

Usage notes

пятьдеся́т (pjatʹdesjat) in the nominative case and accusative case governs the genitive plural of the noun. In other cases, it governs the corresponding plural case of the noun.

  • пятьдеся́т ма́льчиков ― pjatʹdesját málʹčikov ― fifty boys
  • с пятью́десятью ма́льчиками ― s pjatʹjúdesjatʹju málʹčikami ― with fifty boys
  • ма́льчиков пятьдеся́т ― málʹčikov pjatʹdesját ― approximately fifty boys

Synonyms

  • полсо́тни (polsótni) (used in radio conversations to avoid mixing up with шестьдеся́т (šestʹdesját))

Derived terms

  • пятидесятиле́тний (pjatidesjatilétnij)
