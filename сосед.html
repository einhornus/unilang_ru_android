сосе́д (844)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic сусѣдъ (susědŭ, “neighbor”), from Proto-Slavic *sǫsědъ, deverbative of *sъsěděti (“sit together”). The nominal prefix су- (su-) was replaced with productive verbal со- (so-); compare dialectal сусед (suséd). Equivalent to со- (so-) + | -сед (-sed).

Noun[edit]

сосе́д • (soséd) | m | anim (genitive сосе́да, nominative plural сосе́ди, genitive plural сосе́дей, feminine сосе́дка)

  1. neighbour
