<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">smell</strong> (<i>countable and uncountable</i>, <i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">smells</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>A sensation, pleasant or unpleasant, detected by inhaling air (or, the case of water-breathing animals, water) carrying airborne molecules of a substance.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">I love the <b>smell</b> of fresh bread.</i></div></dd></dl>
<ul><li><b>1908</b>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_Grahame" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Kenneth Grahame">Kenneth Grahame</a>, <i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wind_in_the_Willows" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Wind in the Willows">The Wind in the Willows</a></i>
<dl><dd>The penetrating <b>smell</b> of cabbage reached the nose of Toad as he lay prostrate in his misery on the floor, and gave him the idea for a moment that perhaps life was not such a blank and desperate thing as he had imagined. But still he wailed, and kicked with his legs, and refused to be comforted. So the wise girl retired for the time, but, of course, a good deal of the <b>smell</b> of hot cabbage remained behind, as it will do, and Toad, between his sobs, sniffed and reflected, and gradually began to think new and inspiring thoughts: of chivalry, and poetry...</dd></dl></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">physiology</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The sense that detects odours.</li>
<li>A conclusion or intuition that a situation is wrong, more complex than it seems, or otherwise inappropriate.
<ul><li><b>2018</b> Schroers, Carl (<span class="mw-formatted-date" title="2018-02-08">February 8, 2018</span>),  “Chapter 8”, in <cite>Wrestling with Time Lost</cite>, Lulu Press
<dl><dd>“I’m just saying, this has a bad smell to it.”</dd></dl></li></ul></li></ol>
