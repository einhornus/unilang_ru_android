си́ний (721)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *siňь.

Adjective

си́ний • (sínij) (comparative (по)сине́е or (по)сине́й, superlative сине́йший)

  1. deep blue
  2. indigo
  3. (figuratively) dead (sense transferred from the blue color of a dead person's face)
  4. (figuratively) drunken, drunk (also acts as noun)
  5. (slang) being a thief belonging to the Russian mafia (from the blue color of the tattoos that cover his body)

Usage notes

  • си́ний (sínij) and голубо́й (golubój) (different shades of blue) are seen as distinct basic colors in Russian, as different from each other as blue and green would be for an English speaker; Russian does not have one single color term corresponding to blue.

Derived terms

  • (Adjectives) светло́-си́ний (svetló-sínij), тёмно-си́ний (tjómno-sínij), синева́тый (sinevátyj), си́ненький (sínenʹkij), синю́шный (sinjušnyj)
  • (Nouns) синева́ (sinevá), cинь, си́нька (sínʹka), синя́к (sinják)
  • (Verbs) сине́ть (sinétʹ), засинивать (zasinivatʹ)
