опе́ка (13186)


Etymology[edit]

From Polish орiеkа, a calque of Latin prōcūrātor. Compare Czech рéčе, Ukrainian опіка (opíka).

Noun[edit]

опе́ка • (opéka) | f | inan (genitive опе́ки, nominative plural опе́ки, genitive plural опе́к)

  1. guardianship, wardship, tutelage
  2. trusteeship
  3. guardians, board of guardians
  4. care, surveillance

Related terms[edit]

  • опека́ть (opekátʹ)
  • опеку́н (opekún)
  • подопе́чный (podopéčnyj)
