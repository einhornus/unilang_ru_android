<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">day</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">days</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Any period of 24 hours.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">I've been here for two <b>days</b> and a bit.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>A period from midnight to the following midnight.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The <b>day</b> begins at midnight.</i></div></dd>
<dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonyms:</span> <i>see</i> Thesaurus:<span class="Latn" lang="en">day</span></span></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">astronomy</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Rotational period of a planet (especially Earth).
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">A <b>day</b> on Mars is slightly over 24 hours.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>The part of a day period which one spends at one’s job, school, etc.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">I worked two <b>days</b> last week.</i></div></dd></dl>
