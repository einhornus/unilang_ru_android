е́хать (460)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *ěxati.

Verb

е́хать • (jéxatʹ) impf (perfective пое́хать) (concrete)

  1. to go (by horse or vehicle), to ride, to drive
  2. to travel
  3. to move from a place, to slide off

Usage notes

  • Е́хать and пое́хать (pojéxatʹ) are concrete verbs. Their counterpart, е́здить (jézditʹ), is an abstract verb.
  • The imperative forms едь (jedʹ) / е́дьте (jédʹte) and е́хай (jéxaj) / е́хайте (jéxajte) (including derivatives with prefixes) are used but are considered illiterate and are not recommended for use. The recommended imperative forms are suppletive forms поезжа́й (pojezžáj) / поезжа́йте (pojezžájte) but езжа́й (jezžáj) / езжа́йте (jezžájte) are also acceptable. The derivatives (приезжа́ть, заезжа́ть, выезжа́ть, etc.) use prefix + езжа́й (jezžáj) / prefix + езжа́йте (jezžájte).
  • The present adverbial form е́дучи (jéduči) is dated.

Related terms

  • езда́ (jezdá)
