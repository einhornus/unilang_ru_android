ты́сяча (285)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *tysǫti, *tysęti, from Proto-Balto-Slavic *tūˀsantis, from Proto-Indo-European *TuHsont-, *TuHsenti-. Cognate with Ukrainian тисяча (tysjača), Serbo-Croatian тисућа, tisuća, Slovene tisoč, Czech and Slovak tisíc, Polish tysiąc. Akin to Lithuanian tūkstantis, Latvian tūkstotis, Gothic 𐌸𐌿𐍃𐌿𐌽𐌳𐌹 (þusundi).

Numeral

ты́сяча • (týsjača) f inan

  1. thousand (1000)
    ты́сяча ма́льчиков ― týsjača málʹčikov ― a thousand boys
    ма́льчиков ты́сяча ― málʹčikov týsjača ― approximately a thousand boys
    ты́сяча кни́г ― týsjača knig ― a thousand books
    они́ говори́ли о свое́й ты́сяче столо́в
    Oní govoríli o svojéj týsjače stolóv
    They were talking about their thousand tables.

Usage notes

  • ты́сяча (tysjača) in all cases governs the genitive plural of the noun.

Derived terms

  • тысячеле́тие (tysjačelétije)
  • тысячеле́тний (tysjačelétnij)

Noun

ты́сяча • (týsjača) f inan (genitive ты́сячи, nominative plural ты́сячи, genitive plural ты́сяч)

  1. (colloquial) one thousand ruble banknote

Synonyms

  • (colloquial) ты́ща f (týšča)
