мрак (4245)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Old Church Slavonic мракъ (mrakŭ). Doublet of морок (mórok), the inherited East Slavic form.

Noun[edit]

мрак • (mrak) | m | inan (genitive мра́ка, nominative plural мра́ки, genitive plural мра́ков)

  1. shadow, darkness
  2. (genitive) gloom (of), cheerlessness (of)
  3. (predicate; interjection) it's a nightmare, it's appalling/dreadful
  4. (poetic) despair

Synonyms[edit]

  • темнота́ (temnotá)
  • тьма (tʹma)

Related terms[edit]

  • омрача́ть (omračátʹ), омрачи́ть (omračítʹ)
  • омрачаться (omračatʹsja), омрачиться (omračitʹsja)
  • су́мрак (súmrak)
