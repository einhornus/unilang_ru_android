копи́ть (10170)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic копити (kopiti, “to amass, to gather, e.g. people for an army”), from Proto-Slavic *kopiti (“to stack, to pile, e.g. hay”), from *kopa (“heap, pile”), from *kopati, from Proto-Balto-Slavic *kop-, probably ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kep- (“to strike, beat”).

Verb[edit]

копи́ть • (kopítʹ) | impf (perfective накопи́ть)

  1. (transitive) to accumulate, to gather, to amass
  2. to save up (money)
  3. (figuratively) to save up
    копи́ть си́лы ― kopítʹ síly ― to save one's strength
  4. to hide, to conceal (feelings, knowledge, etc.)

Synonyms[edit]

  • (accumulate): нака́пливать (nakáplivatʹ), накоплять (nakopljátʹ), аккумулировать (akkumulírovatʹ), собира́ть (sobirátʹ), сберега́ть (sberegátʹ)
  • (hide): скрыва́ть (skryvátʹ), таи́ть (taítʹ)
