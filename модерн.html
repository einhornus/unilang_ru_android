моде́рн (21237)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from French moderne.

Adjective[edit]

моде́рн • (modɛrn) (indeclinable)

  1. (colloquial) modern, contemporary, fashionable

Usage notes[edit]

  • Normally used either in the post-position or as a predicative adjective.

Synonyms[edit]

  • совреме́нный (sovreménnyj, “contemporary”)
  • мо́дный (módnyj, “fashionable”)
  • модерновый (modɛrnóvyj, “fashionable, contemporary (colloquial)”)

Noun[edit]

моде́рн • (modɛrn) | m | inan (genitive моде́рна, nominative plural моде́рны, genitive plural моде́рнов) or
моде́рн • (modɛrn) | m | inan (indeclinable)

  1. (art) modern style
    стиль моде́рн ― stilʹ modɛrn ― modern style
    в сти́ле моде́рн ― v stíle modɛrn ― in the modern style
    архитекту́ра моде́рна ― arxitektúra modɛrna ― modern architecture

Usage notes[edit]

  • In the post-position the term is also used in the sense of "modern" (adjective)
