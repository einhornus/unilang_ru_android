доро́дный (11208)


Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic дородьныи (dorodĭnyi), from Proto-Slavic *dorodьnъ. Cognate with Ukrainian дорідний (dorídnyj, “productive; tall, large; natural, innate”), Polish dorodny (“healthy; beautiful; mature”). Meanings such as "healthy", "beautiful", "good", "productive", etc. can be found dialectally in Russian. Per ESSJa, from Proto-Slavic *do- + *rodъ + *-ьnъ; compare Russian до- (do-), род (rod), -ный (-nyj).

Adjective[edit]

доро́дный • (doródnyj) (comparative (по)дороднее or (по)дородней)

  1. plump, chubby, corpulent

Synonyms[edit]

  • ро́слый (róslyj), по́лный (pólnyj)

Related terms[edit]

  • дородство (doródstvo)
