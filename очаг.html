оча́г (4140)


Etymology[edit]

From a Turkic source; compare Kyrgyz очок (oçok), Uzbek o'choq, Turkish ocak (“stove”); from Old Turkic očaq | (očaq), očuq | (očuq, “stove, oven”).

Noun[edit]

оча́г • (očág) | m | inan (genitive очага́, nominative plural очаги́, genitive plural очаго́в)

  1. hearth
  2. pocket (an isolated group or area)
    оча́г сопротивле́ния ― očág soprotivlénija ― pocket of resistance
  3. (figuratively) breeding ground, hotbed
    оча́г жёлтой лихора́дки ― očág žóltoj lixorádki ― hotbed of yellow fever

Synonyms[edit]

  • (hearth) ками́н (kamín)
  • (hotbed) рассадник (rassádnik)
