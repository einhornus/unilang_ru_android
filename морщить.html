мо́рщить (15763)


Etymology 1[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *mъrščiti. Compare Bulgarian мръщя (mrǎštja), Czech smrsknout, Slovene mŕščiti, Old Church Slavonic съмърсканъ (sŭmŭrskanŭ), Polish marszczyč, Ukrainian морщити (mórščyty)

Verb[edit]

мо́рщить • (mórščitʹ) | impf (perfective смо́рщить or намо́рщить)

  1. to wrinkle (one's nose)
    Perfective: смо́рщить (smórščitʹ)
  2. to purse (one's lips)
    Perfective: смо́рщить (smórščitʹ)
  3. to furrow (one's forehead, one's brow)
    Perfective: намо́рщить (namórščitʹ)

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

мо́рщить • (morščítʹ) | impf

  1. (colloquial, | intransitive) to crease, to pucker
