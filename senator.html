senator (2170)


Etymology

From senātus (“senate”) + | -tor, originally from senex (“old”).

Noun

senātor m (genitive senātōris); third declension

  1. senator, member of the Roman Senate

Derived terms

  • senāculum
  • senātōrius
  • senātrīx

Related terms

  • senātus
  • seneciō
  • senectus
  • seneō
  • senex
