<head> <meta charset='utf-8'> </head> <body><h1> significant (788)</h1><br><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">significans</i>, present participle of <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">significare</i>, from <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">signum</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">sign</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span> + <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">ficare</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">do, make</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, variant of <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">facere</i>.
</p>
<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adjective">Adjective</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">significant</strong> (<i>comparative</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en comparative-form-of" lang="en">more significant</b>, <i>superlative</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en superlative-form-of" lang="en">most significant</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Signifying something; carrying meaning.
<dl><dd><i>a <b>significant</b> word or sound; a <b>significant</b> look</i></dd></dl>
<ul><li><b>1614</b>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Raleigh" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Walter Raleigh">Walter Raleigh</a>, <i>Historie of the World</i>
<dl><dd>It was well said of Plotinus, that the stars were <b>significant</b>, but not efficient.</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>December 6, 1856</b>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Dickens" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Charles Dickens">Charles Dickens</a>, <i>The Wreck of the Golden Mary</i>
<dl><dd>As evening came on, it grew prematurely dark and cloudy; while the waves acquired that dull indigo tint so <b>significant</b> of ugly weather.</dd></dl></li></ul></li>
<li>Having a covert or hidden meaning.</li>
<li>Having a noticeable or major effect; notable.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>2015</b>,  Shane R. Reeves |  David Wallace,  “The Combatant Status of the “Little Green Men” and Other Participants in the Ukraine Conflict”, in  <cite>International Law Studies, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/US_Naval_War_College" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:US Naval War College">US Naval War College</a></cite> | <sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://digital-commons.usnwc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1321amp;context=ils">[1]</a></sup>, volume 91, number 361, Stockton Center for the Study of International Law, page 393:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">The “little green men”—faces covered, wearing unmarked olive uniforms, speaking Russian and using Russian weapons—have played a <b>significant</b> role in both the occupation of Crimea and the civil war in eastern Ukraine.<sup>196 </sup></span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul>
<dl><dd><i>That was a <b>significant</b> step in the right direction.</i></dd>
<dd><i>The First World War was a <b>significant</b> event.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li>Reasonably large in number or amount.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">statistics</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Having a low probability of occurring by chance (for example, having high correlation and thus likely to be related).</li></ol>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<ul><li>This word may be ambiguous in some situations. In formal writing, care should be taken with comments such as "the difference is significant," because it is not clear without contextual clues whether <i>significant</i> modifies the fact that there is a difference ("notable"), or the difference itself ("large in number or amount"). In some such situations, <i>large</i> and other synonyms may be used in its place.</li></ul>
