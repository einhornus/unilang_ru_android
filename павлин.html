павли́н (14198)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from German Pfäulein, a diminutive of Pfau from Old High German pāwo, from Proto-Germanic *pāwô, itself a borrowing from Latin pāvō.

Noun[edit]

павли́н • (pavlín) | m | anim (genitive павли́на, nominative plural павли́ны, genitive plural павли́нов, feminine пава)

  1. peacock, peafowl, pheasant

Synonyms[edit]

  • пава (pava)

Related terms[edit]

  • павлиний (pavlinij)
