аку́ла (9371)


Etymology

Directly attested since 18th century as а́ккула (ákkula) and аку́л (akúl), while derivative аку́лий (akulij) is used to describe liver oil of a Greenland shark in a source going back to late 16th or early 17th century.

Immediate borrowing from rather different Old Norse hákarl (see internal etymology there) is rejected by Anikin, he rather proposes mediation by some Sami language.

Compare dialectal Norwegian haakall, Skolt Sami akkli, Kildin Sami а̄һклэӈӈк (āhkleŋŋk), Northern Sami áhkạlakkis (“Greenland shark”).

Noun

аку́ла • (akúla) f anim (genitive аку́лы, nominative plural аку́лы, genitive plural аку́л)

  1. shark

Related terms

  • аку́лий (akúlij)
