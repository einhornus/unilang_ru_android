тюфя́к (18112)


Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Tatar tüšäk (“mattress”); dialectally тюшак (tjušák). Modern form probably influenced by тюфя́к (tjufják, “early type of firearm”) (see below).

Noun[edit]

тюфя́к • (tjufják) | m | inan or m | anim (genitive тюфяка́, nominative plural тюфяки́, genitive plural тюфяко́в)

  1. (inanimate) mattress; a sack filled with something soft (e.g. hay) to be used as a mattress
  2. (figuratively, | colloquial, | derogatory, | animate) wimp, weakling, wuss, coward
  3. (engineering) mattress (retaining wall of brushwood, concrete, etc. to protect the river bed and banks of bridges and dams from erosion)
  4. (dialectal, | Siberia, | animate) sandpiper
Synonyms[edit]
  • (mattress): матра́ц (matrác), матра́с (matrás)
