ду́дка (11856)


Etymology[edit]

From дуда (dudá, “fife, pipe”) + | -ка (-ka). Miklosich and Berneker considered this Slavic word to be borrowed from Turkic, but Vasmer and Brückner believe that the close sound rendition of these onomatopoetic words is a "mere chance". Cognate with Lithuanian daudýtė (“panpipe”).

Noun[edit]

ду́дка • (dúdka) | f | inan (genitive ду́дки, nominative plural ду́дки, genitive plural ду́док)

  1. fife, pipe (small shrill pipe)
    пляса́ть под чью-либо ду́дку (idiomatic) ― pljasátʹ pod čʹjú-libo dúdku ― to dance to somebody's tune / piping

Related terms[edit]

  • ду́дки! (dúdki!, “not on your life!, fudge!, rats!”) (interjection)
