ня́ня (6583)


Etymology

Since ancient times, from reduplication in children’s speech. The related form ня́нька (njánʹka) was first attested in the 12th century.

Noun

ня́ня • (njánja) f anim (genitive ня́ни, nominative plural ня́ни, genitive plural ня́нь)

  1. nurse
  2. nanny
  3. babysitter

Related terms

  • ня́нька (njánʹka)
  • ня́нчить (njánčitʹ), ня́нчиться (njančitʹsja)
