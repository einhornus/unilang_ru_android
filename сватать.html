сва́тать (27233)


Etymology[edit]

From Old Church Slavonic сватати (svatati). Compare Ukrainian сватати (svátaty), Belarusian сватаць (svátacʹ), Polish swatać.

Verb[edit]

сва́тать • (svátatʹ) | impf (perfective сосватать)

  1. to propose as a wife or as a husband (as in matchmaking); to match; to marry off

Related terms[edit]

  • сватовство́ (svatovstvó), сватовство́ (svatovstvó)
  • сват (svat), сваха (sváxa), сватья (svátʹja)
