скита́ться (18386)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic скытати ся (skytati sja), cognate with Serbian Church Slavonic скытати сѧ (skytati sę), Ukrainian скитатися (skytátysja), Bulgarian скита́м се (skítam se), Serbo-Croatian скитати се, Slovene skítati sе. More distantly cognate with Lithuanian kutėti (“to shake”), Old Norse skundа (“to hurry”), Old Saxon scuddian (“to shake, to push”), Old High German scutten (“to shake, to push”).

Verb[edit]

скита́ться • (skitátʹsja) | impf

  1. to wander, to stray
    скита́ться | по | белу | све́ту ― skitatʹsja | po | bélu | svétu ― to knock about the world
