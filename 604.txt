<p>Borrowed from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">opus</i>.

<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">opus</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">opuses</b> <i>or</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">opera</b>)
</p>
<ol><li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">music</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A work of music or set of works with a specified rank in an ordering of a composer's complete published works.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">Beethoven's <b>opus</b> eighteen quartets are considered by many to be the beginning of the Romantic era.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>A work, especially of art.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The painter's last <b>opus</b> was a dedication to all things living, in a surprising contrast to all of his prior work.</i></div></dd></dl></li></ol>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p>The most common plural of <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">opus</i> in English is <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">opuses</i>. Some people use the Latin plural, <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">opera</i>. <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">Opi</i> is fairly common in the field of classical music, though mostly in informal contexts. The use of any of these three pluralizations may result in the speaker being corrected, though <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">opi</i>, above all, should be avoided in formal contexts. Outside of music, the word <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">opus</i> sees particularly frequent use in the expression <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">magnum opus</i>.
</p>
