кори́ть (17691)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *korìti. Synchronically, from Old East Slavic ко́р (kor, “swearword, offence”).

Verb[edit]

кори́ть • (korítʹ) | impf

  1. to reproach (with), to upbraid (with, for)
  2. to cast in someone's teeth

Related terms[edit]

  • уко́р (ukór), укори́зна (ukorízna)
  • укори́зненный (ukoríznennyj)
