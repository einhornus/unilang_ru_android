<hr><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun_5">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="fi">kappa</strong>
</p>
<ol><li>An old unit of measure for volume of dry things, still used for potatoes in market squares. A modern <i><b>kappa</b></i> is equal to 5 liters (<i>iso kappa</i>), or 2 liters (<i>pikku kappa</i>), but during the period of autonomy under Russia it was 1/30 of <i class="Latn mention" lang="fi">tynnyri</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">barrel</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span> or 5.4961 liters, and before that under the Swedish rule it was 1/32 of <i>tynnyri</i> or 4.58 liters. A further complication is that Swedish and Russian barrels were not of equal size.</li></ol>
