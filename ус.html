ус (1776)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic усъ (usŭ), from Proto-Slavic *ǫsъ lt; *vǫsъ lt; *vǫdsъ, from Proto-Balto-Slavic *wónsa, from Proto-Indo-European *wondʰsom, s-expansion of root *wendʰ- (“hair, wool, beard”).

Noun[edit]

ус • (us) | m | inan (genitive у́са, nominative plural усы́, genitive plural усо́в)

  1. (usually | in the plural) moustache
  2. whisker
  3. feeler, antenna
  4. tendril, awn

Related terms[edit]

  • уса́тый (usátyj)
  • усач (usač), у́сик (úsik)
