серп (11539)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *sьrpъ, from Proto-Indo-European *sr̥p-. Cognate with {{cog|ru|Old Church Slavonic срьпъ (srĭpŭ, “sickle”), Bulgarian сърп (sǎrp), Czech srp, Polish sierp, Yiddish סערפּ | (serp); and with Latvian sirpis, Greek ἅρπη (ἅrpi), Latin sarpō.

Noun[edit]

серп • (serp) | m | inan (genitive серпа́, nominative plural серпы́, genitive plural серпо́в)

  1. sickle
    серп и мо́лот ― sérp i mólot ― hammer and sickle
  2. crescent
