баклажа́н (26923)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ottoman Turkish بادلجان | (bâdlicân) under influence of баклага (baklága), from Persian بادنجان | (bâdenjân), from earlier بادنگان | (bâdengân), from Indo-Aryan, likely ultimately from Dravidian. Compare Sanskrit भण्डाकी (bhaṇḍākī).

Noun[edit]

баклажа́н • (baklažán) | m | inan (genitive баклажа́на, nominative plural баклажа́ны, genitive plural баклажа́нов or баклажа́н*, related adjective баклажановый) (* Informal.)

  1. aubergine (plant and vegetable)
