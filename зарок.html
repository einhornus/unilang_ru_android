заро́к (29160)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *zarokъ. Deverbal from early Proto-Slavic *zarekti (later *zareťi), with Indo-European ablaut typical of nouns. Compare заречь (zaréčʹ, “to prohibit, to force to renounce”), заречься (zaréčʹsja, “to renounce, to forswear”).

Noun[edit]

заро́к • (zarók) | m | inan (genitive заро́ка, nominative plural заро́ки, genitive plural заро́ков)

  1. negative oath or vow (solemn promise not to do something)

Related terms[edit]

  • зарекать | impf (zarekátʹ), заречь | pf (zaréčʹ)
  • зарекаться | impf (zarekátʹsja), заречься | pf (zaréčʹsja)
