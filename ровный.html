ро́вный (2161)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *orvьnъ. Cognate with Belarusian роўны (róŭny). Doublet of ра́вный (rávnyj), a borrowing from Old Church Slavonic.

Adjective[edit]

ро́вный • (róvnyj) (comparative (по)ровнее or (по)ро́вней, superlative ровнейший)

  1. even, level, flat, smooth
  2. straight
  3. equal, balanced

Usage notes[edit]

  • A special short masculine singular form is found in the expression не ровён час (ne rovjón čás, “God forbid, you never know”, literally “the hour is not even”).
