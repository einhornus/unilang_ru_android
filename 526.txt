<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<ul><li><i><b>Many</b></i> is used only with the plural of countable nouns (except in the combination <i>many a</i>). Its counterpart used with uncountable nouns is <i>much</i>. <i>Many</i> and <i>much</i> merge in the comparative and superlative forms, which are <i>more</i> and <i>most</i> for both determiners.</li></ul>
<ul><li>It was once common to use the indefinite article with <i>many</i> (<i>very a many years ago</i>), as it still is with <i>few</i> (<i>a few good men</i>). However, this has fallen out of favor except in formations such as "a great/good many."</li></ul>
