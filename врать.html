врать (1626)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *vьrati, 1sg. *vьrǫ (as a verb, only preserved in Russian and Serbo-Croatian). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *werh₁- (“to speak”). Cognate with Serbo-Croatian varati (“to betray”), Lithuanian var̃das (“name”), Latvian vārds (“name; word”), Latin verbum (“word”), English word, Ancient Greek εἴρω (eírō, “to speak”), ῥήτωρ (rhḗtōr, “orator”) (compare English rhetoric, borrowed from Greek), Hittite [script needed] (weriya-, “to call, to name”), Sanskrit व्रत (vratá, “pledge, vow”).

Verb[edit]

врать • (vratʹ) | impf (perfective совра́ть)

  1. to lie
  2. to make a mistake
  3. to be inaccurate
  4. to tell tales

Synonyms[edit]

  • лгать (lgatʹ)

Related terms[edit]

  • враки (vraki)
  • враль (vralʹ)
  • врач (vrač)
  • враньё (vranʹjó)
  • врун (vrun)
