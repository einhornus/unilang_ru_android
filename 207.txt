<head> <meta charset='utf-8'> </head> <body><h1> cue (5713)</h1><br><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_1">Etymology 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p>From the letter Q, abbreviation of <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">quando</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">when</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, marked on actor's play copy where they were to begin.
</p>
<hr><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">cue</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">cues</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>An action or event that is a signal for somebody to do something.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>2011</b> November 3,  Chris Bevan,  “Rubin Kazan 1 - 0 Tottenham”, in  <cite>BBC Sport</cite> | <sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/europe/15472973.stm">[1]</a></sup>:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">This time Cudicini was left helpless when Natcho stepped up to expertly curl the ball into the top corner.<br />That was the <b>cue</b> for further pressure from the Russian side and it took further Cudicini saves to keep the score down.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li>The last words of a play actor's speech, serving as an intimation for the next actor to speak; any word or words which serve to remind an actor to speak or to do something; a catchword.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><i><abbr title="circa">c.</abbr></i> <b>1595–1596</b>,  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare" class="extiw" title="w:William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>,  “<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" class="extiw" title="w:A Midsummer Night | s Dream">A Midsommer Nights Dreame</a>”, in  <cite>Mr. William Shakespeares Comedies, Histories, amp; Tragedies: Published According to the True Originall Copies</cite> (<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Folio" class="extiw" title="w:First Folio">First Folio</a>), London |  Printed by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Jaggard" class="extiw" title="w:William Jaggard">Isaac Iaggard</a>, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Blount" class="extiw" title="w:Edward Blount">Ed[ward] Blount</a>, published 1623, <small><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OCLC" class="extiw" title="w:OCLC">OCLC</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://worldcat.org/oclc/606515358">606515358</a></small>,  | Act IV, scene i | :</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">When my <b>cue</b> comes, call me, and I will answer.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li>A hint or intimation.
<ul><li><b>1731</b>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonathan_Swift" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Jonathan Swift">Jonathan Swift</a>, <i><a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Works_of_the_Rev._Jonathan_Swift/Volume_16/Directions_to_Servants" class="extiw" title="s:The Works of the Rev. Jonathan Swift/Volume 16/Directions to Servants">Directions to Servants</a></i>
<dl><dd>Give them [the servants] their <b>cue</b> to attend in two lines as he leaves the house.</dd></dl></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">obsolete</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Humour; temper of mind.</li>
<li><span class="use-with-mention">The name of the Latin-script letter <span class="mention">Q</span></span>.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">obsolete<span class="ib-comma">,</span> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Britain" class="extiw" title="w:Great Britain">Britain</a><span class="ib-comma">,</span> universities</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A small portion of bread or beer; the quantity bought with a farthing or half farthing and noted with a <i>q</i> (for <span class="etylcleanup"><span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">quadrans</i> farthing) in the buttery books.</li></ol>
