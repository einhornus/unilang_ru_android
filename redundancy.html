redundancy (8896)


ед. ч. мн. ч.
redundancy redundancies

Существи́тельное

Значе́ние

  1. чрезмерность; избыточность
    • Redundancy, or the number of alternate paths in the network, is an important part of providing reliability in the network.
    • Redundancy occurs when the same information is presented in multiple forms or is unnecessarily elaborated.
  2. изли́шек рабо́чей си́лы
  3. сокраще́ние шта́та; увольне́ние рабо́чих или слу́жащих
    • Organisational change can take many forms and as a result employers may be required to effect redundancies.
    • A redundancy occurs when you dismiss someone because your requirement for the work that person was doing has ceased or reduced in that location.
  4. многословие

Сино́нимы

  1. redundance, superfluity, excess
