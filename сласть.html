сласть (28315)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Old Church Slavonic сласть (slastĭ), from Proto-Slavic *solstь. The native Old East Slavic *солость (*solostĭ) is unattested, but is the source of солостить (solostítʹ).

Noun[edit]

сласть • (slastʹ) | f | inan (genitive сла́сти, nominative plural сла́сти, genitive plural сласте́й)

  1. (colloquial, | rare) sweetness
  2. (colloquial) pleasure, enjoyment, candies
  3. (plural only) sweets
