лафа́ (29584)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Arabic عَلَف | (ʿalaf, “fodder”), from Old East Slavic алафа (alafa) or олафа (olafa). Cognates include Ukrainian лахва (laxvá) and Belarusian лахва (laxvá).

Noun[edit]

лафа́ • (lafá) | f | inan (genitive лафы́, nominative plural лафы́, genitive plural лаф)

  1. (dated, | colloquial) benefit; luck; enjoyment
    У него́ сейча́с не жизнь, а лафа́. ― U nevó sejčás ne žiznʹ, a lafá. ― His life is like a holiday now.
