морок (28190)


Etymology 1[edit]

By pleophony from Proto-Slavic *morkъ. Doublet of мрак (mrak), a borrowing from Old Church Slavonic.

Noun[edit]

морок • (mórok) | m | inan (genitive морока, uncountable)

  1. (dialectal) darkness
  2. (dialectal) clouds, fog
Synonyms[edit]
  • мрак (mrak), су́мрак (súmrak), темнота́ (temnotá), ма́рево (márevo), мгла (mgla)
  • о́бморок (óbmorok), припа́док (pripádok)

Etymology 2[edit]

Noun[edit]

морок • (morók) | f | inan | pl

  1. genitive plural of морока (moróka)
