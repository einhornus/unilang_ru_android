<head> <meta charset='utf-8'> </head> <body><h1> corral (18046)</h1><br><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language" class="extiw" title="w:Spanish language">Spanish</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="es">corral</i>. Doublet of <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">kraal</i>.
</p>
<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">corral</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">corrals</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>An enclosure for livestock, especially a circular one.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">We had a small <b>corral</b> out back where we kept our pet llama.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>An enclosure or area to concentrate a dispersed group.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">Please return the shopping carts to the <b>corral</b>.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>A circle of wagons, either for the purpose of trapping livestock, or for defense.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The wagon train formed a <b>corral</b> to protect against Comanche attacks.</i></div></dd></dl></li></ol>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Synonyms">Synonyms</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<ul><li><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content qualifier-content">livestock enclosure</span><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">)</span><span class="ib-colon sense-qualifier-colon">:</span> pen, stockade</li></ul>
<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Verb">Verb</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">corral</strong> (<i>third-person singular simple present</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en 3 | s | pres-form-of" lang="en">corrals</b>, <i>present participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en pres | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">corralling</b> <i>or</i> <span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content qualifier-content">US</span><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">)</span> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en pres | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">corraling</b>, <i>simple past and past participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en past | and | past | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">corralled</b> <i>or</i> <span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content qualifier-content">US</span><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">)</span> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en past | and | past | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">corraled</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>To capture or round up.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The lawyer frantically tried to <b>corral</b> his notes as his briefcase fell open.</i></div></dd>
<dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">Between us, we managed to <b>corral</b> the puppy in the kitchen.</i></div></dd></dl>
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>1898</b>,  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winston_Churchill_(novelist)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Winston Churchill (novelist)">Winston Churchill</a>,  chapter 8, in  <cite><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Celebrity" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Celebrity">The Celebrity</a></cite>:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">I <b>corralled</b> the judge, and we started off across the fields, in no very mild state of fear of that gentleman's wife, whose vigilance was seldom relaxed. And thus we came by a circuitous route to Mohair, the judge occupied by his own guilty thoughts, and I by others not less disturbing.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li>
<li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>1964</b> March,  “News and Comment: Coal concentration in Birmingham”, in  <cite>Modern Railways</cite>, page 152:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">By the end of this year the work of 168 coal depots scattered throughout the Birmingham Division will have been <b>coralled</b> [sic] into about two dozen concentration depots.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li>
<li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>2019</b> November 16,  Austin Ramzy |  Chris Buckley,  “‘Absolutely No Mercy’: Leaked Files Expose How China Organized Mass Detentions of Muslims”, in  <cite>New York Times</cite> | <sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2019/11/16/world/asia/china-xinjiang-documents.html">[1]</a></sup>:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">They provide an unprecedented inside view of the continuing clampdown in Xinjiang, in which the authorities have <b>corralled</b> as many as a million ethnic Uighurs, Kazakhs and others into internment camps and prisons over the past three years.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li>To place inside of a corral.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">After we <b>corralled</b> the last steer, we headed off to the chuck wagon for dinner.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>To make a circle of vehicles, as of wagons so as to form a corral.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The cattle drivers <b>corralled</b> their wagons for the night.</i></div></dd></dl></li></ol>
