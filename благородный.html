благоро́дный (2024)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Old Church Slavonic благородьнъ (blagorodĭnŭ). Synchronically analyzable as бла́го (blágo, “good”) + | -о- (-o-) + | род (rod, “birth”) + | -ный (-nyj).

Adjective[edit]

благоро́дный • (blagoródnyj) (comparative (по)благороднее or (по)благородней, superlative благороднейший)

  1. noble, aristocratic (of the nobility, of the high-born class, especially in Russia before 1917)
  2. noble (in spirit), gentle
    благоро́дный посту́пок ― blagoródnyj postúpok ― noble act
  3. selfless, generous
  4. exceptional, outstandingly elegant
    благоро́дная красота́ ― blagoródnaja krasotá ― exceptional beauty
  5. (sciences, | figuratively) noble (having some special quality)
    благоро́дный газ ― blagoródnyj gáz ― noble gas, inert gas

Noun[edit]

благоро́дный • (blagoródnyj) | m | anim (genitive благоро́дного, nominative plural благоро́дные, genitive plural благоро́дных, feminine благоро́дная)

  1. noble, aristocrat (member of the nobility, of the high-born class, especially in Russia before 1917)
  2. someone noble in spirit

Related terms[edit]

  • благоро́дство (blagoródstvo)
