у́хо (393)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *uxo, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ṓws. Cognate with Old Armenian ունկն (unkn), Gothic 𐌰𐌿𐍃𐍉 (ausō), Ancient Greek οὖς (oûs), Latin auris, Persian هوش | (hūš, hōš, “intellect”).

Noun[edit]

у́хо • (úxo) | n | inan (genitive уха, nominative plural у́ши, genitive plural уше́й)

  1. ear
    прикладывать/приложить часы́ к уху ― prikládyvatʹ/priložítʹ časý k úxu ― to put a watch to one's ear
