язы́чник (22618)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic ꙗзꙑчьникъ (jazyčĭnikŭ), from ꙗзꙑкъ (jazykŭ, “tongue; nation”). Modeled on Ancient Greek ἔθνος (éthnos, “nation”) | : ἐθνικός (ethnikós, “heathen”).

Noun[edit]

язы́чник • (jazýčnik) | m | anim (genitive язы́чника, nominative plural язы́чники, genitive plural язы́чников, feminine язычница)

  1. pagan, heathen (not adhering to an Abrahamic religion)
  2. polytheist

Synonyms[edit]

  • (polytheist): политеист (politɛíst), многобожец (mnogobóžec)
