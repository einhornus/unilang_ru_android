о́сень (1485)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic о́сень (osenĭ), from Proto-Slavic *(j)esenь, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁es-en-, whence English earn and German Ernte (“harvest”).

Doublet of есень (jesenʹ) and есеня (jesenja), cognate with Belarusian восень (vósjenʹ) and Ukrainian осінь (ósinʹ).

Noun[edit]

о́сень • (ósenʹ) | f | inan (genitive осени, nominative plural осени, genitive plural о́сеней)
