доброду́шный (7009)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic добродꙋшьнꙑи (dobrodušĭnyi), from добро (dobro, “good”) + дꙋша (duša, “soul”) + -ьнꙑи (-ĭnyi), a calque of Ancient Greek εὔψυχος (eúpsukhos). Also, compare with Macedonian доброду́шен (dobrodušen), Serbo-Croatian добродушан, Czech dobrodušný, Polish dobroduszny.

Adjective[edit]

доброду́шный • (dobrodúšnyj)

  1. good-natured

Related terms[edit]
