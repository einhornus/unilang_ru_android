уто́к (5994)


Etymology 1[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic утъкъ (utŭkŭ), from Proto-Slavic *ǫtъkъ, from *ǫ- (Russian у- (u-)) + *tъkъ, cognate with or deverbal from *tъkati (Russian ткать (tkatʹ)).

Noun[edit]

уто́к • (utók) | m | inan (genitive утка́, nominative plural утки́, genitive plural утко́в)

  1. weft, woof

Etymology 2[edit]

Noun[edit]

уто́к • (útok) | f | anim | pl

  1. genitive plural of утка́ (útka)
  2. accusative plural of утка́ (útka)
