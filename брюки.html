брю́ки (1893)


Etymology

Borrowed from Dutch broek. First attested in the eighteenth century, but became more popular a hundred years later. Compare English breeches.

Noun

брю́ки • (brjúki) f inan pl (genitive брю́к, plural only)

  1. pants, trousers

Related terms

  • брю́чки f (brjúčki)

брю́-ки
