и́кать (18472)


Etymology 1[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *jьkati, morphologically from onomatopoeic и (i) + | -кать (-katʹ).

Verb[edit]

и́кать • (ikátʹ) | impf (perfective икну́ть)

  1. to hiccup
Related terms[edit]
  • ико́та | f (ikóta)

Etymology 2[edit]

и (i) + | -кать (-katʹ)

Verb[edit]

и́кать • (íkatʹ) | impf

  1. (linguistics) to pronounce unstressed е (je) as и (i), to the Standard Russian pronunciation, to rather than as е (je)
Usage notes[edit]

An example is the Standard Russian pronunciation of земля́ (zemljá) as [zʲɪmˈlʲa], which could be respelled as *зимля (*zimljá).
