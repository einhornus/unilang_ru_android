соха́ (20823)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *soxa, akin to Bulgarian соха́ (sohá, “forked rod”), Serbo-Croatian соха́/soha, Slovak socha (“statue”). Non-Slavic cognates include Sanskrit शाखा (śākhā, “branch”), Gothic 𐌷𐍉𐌷𐌰 (hōha, “plough”), Armenian ցախ (cʿax, “branch”), Lithuanian šakà (“branch”), Old Irish géc (“branch”) and Persian شاخ | (šâx, “branch”).

Noun[edit]

соха́ • (soxá) | f | inan (genitive сохи, nominative plural сохи, genitive plural со́х, diminutive со́шка)

  1. wooden plow/plough
