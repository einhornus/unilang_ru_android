чиха́ть (11963)


Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Proto-Slavic *čixati, of onomatopoeic origin. Cognate with Bulgarian чихамь (číhamʹ) (Gerov), Slovene číhati (tonal orthography) (1sg. čȋham (tonal orthography)), Upper Sorbian čichać, Lower Sorbian tśichaś. Also found as чхать (čxatʹ), inherited from Proto-Slavic *čьxati, cognate with Ukrainian чхати (čxáty), Belarusian чхаць (čxacʹ), Polish czchać.

Verb[edit]

чиха́ть • (čixátʹ) | impf (perfective чихну́ть)

  1. to sneeze
  2. to scorn, to disdain
    мне чиха́ть (на)
    mne čixátʹ (na)
    I don't give a damn (for), I don't care a button (about)

Related terms[edit]

  • чих (čix)

  • чихательный (čixatelʹnyj)
