глода́ть (21353)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *glodati; cognate with Ukrainian глодати (hlodáty), Bulgarian гложда (glóžda), Serbo-Croatian гло̀дати, Slovene glódati, Czech hlodat, Polish głodać, Upper Sorbian hłodać, Lower Sorbian głodaś (“to scratch”). More distantly cognate with Old Norse glata (“to destroy”).

Verb[edit]

глода́ть • (glodátʹ) | impf

  1. to gnaw

Verb[edit]

глода́ть • (glodátʹ) | impf

  1. to torment, to gnaw at (of feelings)
