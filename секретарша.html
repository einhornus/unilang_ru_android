секрета́рша (4494)


Etymology

From секрета́рь (sekretárʹ) + | -ша (-ša).

Noun

секрета́рша • (sekretárša) f anim (genitive секрета́рши, nominative plural секрета́рши, genitive plural секрета́рш, masculine секрета́рь)

  1. (colloquial) secretary (female)
  2. (colloquial) the wife of a secretary
    колле́жская секрета́рша ― kolléžskaja sekretárša ― the wife of the collegiate secretary

Usage notes

  • Some occupation names work for both males and females. The male form of the term may refer to a male or a female. This is a more colloquial term and is likely to be negatively perceived.

сек-ре-та́р-ша
