<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English" class="extiw" title="w:Middle English">Middle English</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">moneie</i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">moneye</i>, borrowed from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French" class="extiw" title="w:Old French">Old French</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro">moneie</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">money</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">monēta</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">money, a place for coining money, coin, mint</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, from the name of the temple of Juno Moneta in Rome, where a mint was. </p>
<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">money</strong> (<i>usually uncountable</i>, <i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">monies</b> <i>or</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">moneys</b>) (plural used only in certain senses)
</p>
<ol><li>A legally or socially binding conceptual contract of entitlement to wealth, void of intrinsic value, payable for all debts and taxes, and regulated in supply.</li>
<li>A generally accepted means of exchange and measure of value.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>1913</b>,  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_C._Lincoln" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Joseph C. Lincoln">Joseph C. Lincoln</a>,  chapter 1, in  <cite><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://openlibrary.org/works/OL5535161W">Mr. Pratt's Patients</a></cite>:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">Then there came a reg'lar terror of a sou'wester same as you don't get one summer in a thousand, and blowed the shanty flat and ripped about half of the weir poles out of the sand. We spent consider'ble <b>money</b> getting 'em reset, and then a swordfish got into the pound and tore the nets all to slathers, right in the middle of the squiteague season.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li>
<li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>2013</b> August 10,  “<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.economist.com/news/leaders/21583277-worlds-biggest-polluter-going-green-it-needs-speed-up-transition-can-china">Can China clean up fast enough?</a>”, in  <cite><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Economist" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Economist">The Economist</a></cite>, volume 408, number 8848:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">At the same time, it is pouring <b>money</b> into cleaning up the country.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul>
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">Before colonial times cowry shells imported from Mauritius were used as <b>money</b> in Western Africa.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>A currency maintained by a state or other entity which can guarantee its value (such as a monetary union).
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en"><b>money</b> supply;  <b>money</b> market</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>Hard cash in the form of banknotes and coins, as opposed to cheques/checks, credit cards, or credit more generally.</li>
<li>The total value of liquid assets available for an individual or other economic unit, such as cash and bank deposits.</li>
<li>Wealth; a person, family or class that possesses wealth
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">He was born with <b>money</b>.</i></div> <div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">He married <b>money</b>.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li>An item of value between two or more parties used for the exchange of goods or services.</li>
<li>A person who funds an operation.</li></ol>
