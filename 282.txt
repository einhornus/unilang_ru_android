<head> <meta charset='utf-8'> </head> <body><h1> em (4074)</h1><br><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_1">Etymology 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p>The typographic em is named after the metal type for the capital <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">M</i> in early printing, whose body was square (the printed letter M is almost never one em in width).
</p>
<hr><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">em</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">ems</b>)
</p>
<ol><li><span class="use-with-mention">The name of the Latin-script letter <span class="mention">M</span></span>.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The <b>ems</b> and ens at the beginnings and ends.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">typography</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A unit of measurement equal to the height of the type in use.
<dl><dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonyms:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">quad</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">em quad</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">mutton</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">mut</span></span></dd></dl></li></ol>
