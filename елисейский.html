елисе́йский (23900)


Etymology[edit]

From Элисий (Elísij, “Elysium”) + | -ский (-skij). Элисий (Elísij) is a less common form of Элизий (Elízij), also Элизиум (Elízium). The former term was borrowed directly from Ancient Greek Ἠλύσιον (Ēlúsion, “Elysian”), while the latter two terms came via Latin, in turn from the same Greek word.

Adjective[edit]

елисе́йский • (jeliséjskij) (no comparative)

  1. Elysian, paradisiacal
    елисе́йские поля ― jeliséjskije poljá ― Champs-Élysées (famous avenue in Paris)

Related terms[edit]

  • Элисий (Elísij), Элизий (Elízij), Элизиум (Elízium)
