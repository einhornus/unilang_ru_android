пету́х (3763)


Etymology[edit]

петь (petʹ) + | -ух (-ux), said to have been influenced by Петя (Pétja), diminutive of Пётр (Pjotr). Related to петь (petʹ, “to sing”). Compare певень (pévenʹ), Ukrainian півень (pívenʹ), Belarusian певень (pjévjenʹ), Bulgarian петел (petél), Macedonian петел (petel), Serbo-Croatian pijétao, pijévac, Slovene petêlin, pẹtəł. Compare also Polish piać (“to crow”), which is cognate with петь (petʹ). Likewise | Latin canere means both “to sing” and “to crow”.
