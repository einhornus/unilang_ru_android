<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English" class="extiw" title="w:Middle English">Middle English</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">grand</i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">grond</i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">graund</i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">graunt</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Norman_language" class="extiw" title="w:Anglo-Norman language">Anglo-Norman</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro">graunt</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French" class="extiw" title="w:Old French">Old French</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="fro">grant</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Latin">Latin</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">grandis</i>. Doublet of <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">grande</i> and <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">grandee</i>.
</p>
<hr><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Adjective">Adjective</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">grand</strong> (<i>comparative</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en comparative-form-of" lang="en">grander</b> <i>or</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en comparative-form-of" lang="en">more grand</b>, <i>superlative</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en superlative-form-of" lang="en">grandest</b> <i>or</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en superlative-form-of" lang="en">most grand</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Of a large size or extent; great.
<dl><dd><i>a <b>grand</b> mountain</i></dd>
<dd><i>a <b>grand</b> army</i></dd>
<dd><i>a <b>grand</b> mistake</i></dd></dl></li>
<li>Great in size, and fine or imposing in appearance or impression; illustrious, dignified, magnificent.
<dl><dd><i>a <b>grand</b> monarch</i></dd>
<dd><i>a <b>grand</b> view</i></dd>
<dd><i>His simple vision has transformed into something far more <b>grand</b>.</i></dd></dl>
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>1863</b>,  Sheridan Le Fanu,  <cite>The House by the Churchyard</cite>:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">In the mean time, Cluffe had arrived. He was a little bit huffed and <b>grand</b> at being nailed as an evidence, upon a few words carelessly, or, if you will, confidentially dropped at his own mess-table, where Lowe chanced to be a guest; and certainly with no suspicion that his little story could in any way be made to elucidate the mystery of Sturk's murder.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li>Having higher rank or more dignity, size, or importance than other persons or things of the same name.
<dl><dd><i>a <b>grand</b> lodge</i></dd>
<dd><i>a <b>grand</b> vizier</i></dd>
<dd><i>a <b>grand</b> piano</i></dd>
<dd><i>The Grand Viziers of the Ottoman Empire.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content qualifier-content">usually in compound forms</span><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">)</span> Standing in the second or some more remote degree of parentage or descent.
<dl><dd><i>grandfather, grandson, grand-child</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ireland" class="extiw" title="w:Ireland">Ireland</a><span class="ib-comma">,</span> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_England" class="extiw" title="w:Northern England">Northern England</a><span class="ib-comma">,</span> colloquial<span class="ib-comma">,</span> otherwise  dated</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Fine; lovely.
<dl><dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">A cup of tea? That'd be <b>grand</b>.</i></div></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">music</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Containing all the parts proper to a given form of composition.</li></ol>
