ма́монт (13671)


Etymology[edit]

Probably from an Uralic language, like Proto-Mansi *mē̮ŋ-ońt (“earth-horn”) (compare Northern Mansi ма (ma, “earth”), аньт (anʹt, “horn”)) or Khanty. Compare Proto-Uralic *mëxe (“earth”).

Noun[edit]

ма́монт • (mámont) | m | anim (genitive ма́монта, nominative plural ма́монты, genitive plural ма́монтов, related adjective мамонтовый)

  1. mammoth
