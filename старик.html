ста́рик (314)


Etymology[edit]

From старий (starij). Cognates include Czech stařík.

Noun[edit]

ста́рик • (starík) | m | anim (genitive ста́рика, nominative plural ста́рики, genitive plural стариков, feminine стару́ха)

  1. old man
  2. (slang) old-timer, that man who has worked or served somewhere more than his younger colleagues
  3. (colloquial) father, foozle
  4. (colloquial) elderly husband for a wife
  5. (colloquial, | address) chap
