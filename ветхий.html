ве́тхий (9306)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic ветъхъ (vetŭxŭ), from Proto-Slavic *vetъxъ (“old, ancient”).

Adjective[edit]

ве́тхий • (vétxij) (rare/awkward comparative (по)ветше)

  1. (dated) ancient, old
    Ве́тхий Завет ― Vétxij Zavét ― Old Testament
  2. old, dilapidated, shabby, ramshackle, decrepit, rickety

Related terms[edit]

  • ве́тошь (vétošʹ)
