<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">malt</strong> (<i>countable and uncountable</i>, <i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">malts</b>)
</p>
<ol><li><a href="Verb">Malted</a> grain <span class="gloss-brac">(</span><span class="gloss-content">sprouted grain</span><span class="gloss-brac">)</span> (usually barley), used in brewing and otherwise.</li>
<li>Malt liquor, especially malt whisky.
<ul><li><b>1896</b>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A._E._Housman" class="extiw" title="w:A. E. Housman">A. E. Housman</a>, <i>A Shropshire Lad</i>, LXII:
<dl><dd>Oh many a peer of England brews</dd>
<dd>Livelier liquor than the Muse,</dd>
<dd>And <b>malt</b> does more than Milton can</dd>
<dd>To justify God's ways to man.</dd></dl></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States" class="extiw" title="w:United States">US</a><span class="ib-comma">,</span> informal</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A milkshake with malted milk powder added for flavor.
<dl><dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonym:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">malted</span></span></dd></dl></li>
<li>Maltose-rich sugar derived from malted grain.</li></ol>
