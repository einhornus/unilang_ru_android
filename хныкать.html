хны́кать (22608)


Etymology[edit]

Onomatopoetic in origin. Compare dialectal хмы́кать (xmýkatʹ, “to whine, to whimper”), Ukrainian хникати (xnýkaty, “to whine, to whimper”), and similar verbs in other languages, such as Czech kníkati (“to whine, to squeal”), also kničeti, kňučeti; Polish knychać (“to whine, to squeal”); Latvian šnukstêt (“to sob, to whine”), dialectal German schnucken (“to sigh, to sob, to whimper”).

Verb[edit]

хны́кать • (xnýkatʹ) | impf

  1. (colloquial) to whine, to whimper
  2. (colloquial, | figuratively) to complain tearfully, to sob
