манипуля́тор (28484)


Etymology[edit]

манипул(ировать) (manipul(írovatʹ)) + | -ятор (-játor)

Noun[edit]

манипуля́тор • (manipuljátor) | m | anim or m | inan (genitive манипуля́тора, nominative plural манипуля́торы, genitive plural манипуля́торов)

  1. manipulator (person who manipulates others)
  2. circus performer who performs sleight of hand tricks, prestidigitator
  3. manipulator (mechanical device for performing complex operations)
  4. (telegraphy) telegraph key (for entering Morse code)
  5. (computing) external pointing device (e.g. a mouse or trackball)

Related terms[edit]

  • манипули́ровать (manipulírovatʹ)
  • манипуля́ция (manipuljácija)
