пра́во (277)


Etymology 1[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic пра́во (pravo). Calque of German Recht and French droit, based on пра́вый (právyj, “right, correct; straight”). Cognate with Ukrainian пра́во (právo), Belarusian права (práva), Old Church Slavonic пра́во (pravo), Bulgarian пра́во (právo), Serbo-Croatian пра̏во, Slovene prȃvo (tonal orthography), Czech právo, Slovak právo, Polish prawo.

Noun[edit]

пра́во • (právo) | n | inan (genitive права, nominative plural права, genitive plural пра́в)

  1. right; claim
  2. (law) law, right
  3. justice
  4. (in the plural) license

Etymology 2[edit]

пра́вый (právyj) + | -о (-o)

Adverb[edit]

пра́во • (právo)

  1. indeed, really

Adjective[edit]

пра́во • (právo)

  1. short neuter singular of пра́вый (právyj)
