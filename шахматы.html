ша́хматы (6209)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic ша́хматы (šaxmaty), probably from Middle High German schāch unde mat (compare German Schachmatt (“checkmate”)), from Persian شاه مات | (šâh mât, “the king is conquered”).

Noun[edit]

ша́хматы • (šáxmaty) | f | inan | pl (genitive ша́хмат, plural only)

  1. chess
