каза́к (3870)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic козак (kozak), from Turkic languages ("free, independent person, an adventurer, a drifter"), cognate to каза́х (kazáx, “Kazakh”).

Noun[edit]

каза́к • (kazák) | m | anim (genitive казака́, nominative plural каза́ки or каза́ки, genitive plural каза́ков or каза́ков, feminine казачка́)

  1. Cossack, cossack
