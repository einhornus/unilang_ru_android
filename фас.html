фас (21189)


Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from French face (“face, front part”).

Noun[edit]

фас • (fas) | m | inan (genitive фа́са, nominative plural фа́сы, genitive plural фа́сов)

  1. front, face
    в фас ― v fas ― fullface
    сфотографироваться в фас ― sfotografírovatʹsja v fas ― have one's picture taken fullface
    портрет/фотография в фас ― portrét/fotográfija v fas ― fullfaced picture
Related terms[edit]
  • анфас (anfás)

Etymology 2[edit]

Borrowed from German fass (“catch, snatch (imperative form)”).

Interjection[edit]

фас • (fas)

  1. (a command to a dog) attack!, get'em!, bite!
