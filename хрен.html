хрен (2592)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic хрѣнъ (xrěnŭ)?, from Proto-Slavic *xrěnъ (“horseradish”).

Noun[edit]

хрен • (xren) | m | inan or m | anim (genitive хре́на, nominative plural хре́ны, genitive plural хре́нов, related adjective хрено́вый)

  1. (inanimate) horseradish (plant, condiment)
  2. (inanimate, | euphemistic, | colloquial) cock, dick (for vulgar idioms and literal uses of хер (xer) or хуй (xuj))
    Ни хре́на не понима́ю, как э́то собра́ть. (note the stress)
    Ni xrená ne ponimáju, kak éto sobrátʹ.
    I understand bugger-all about how to put this together.
    Како́го хре́на он пришёл? (note the stress)
    Kakóvo xréna on prišól?
    What the fuck did he come for?
  3. (animate, | derogatory, | colloquial) old fart

Synonyms[edit]

  • (euphemisms): шиш (šiš), фи́г (fig)
  • (old fart): хрыч (xryč), ста́рый хрыч (stáryj xryč)
