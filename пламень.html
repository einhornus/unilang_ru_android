пла́мень (27541)


Etymology[edit]

Variant of пла́мя (plámja), a borrowing from Old Church Slavonic. Compare Bulgarian пламён (plámen), Serbo-Croatian пла̏ме̄н, Slovene plámen (gen. sg. plaména)), Czech plamen, Slovak plameň, Polish płomień.

Noun[edit]

пла́мень • (plámenʹ) | m | inan

  1. (folk poetic) flame (also figurative, referring to emotions)

Usage notes[edit]

Exists only in the nominative and accusative singular.
