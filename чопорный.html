чо́порный (26140)


Etymology[edit]

From an unattested Old East Slavic word + -ный (-nyj); compare Russian Church Slavic чепрь (čeprĭ, “delight, pleasure”). Also cognate with чепориться (čeporítʹsja, “to be important”) (also чепуриться (čepurítʹsja), чепуриться (čepúritʹsja)), and similarly with Ukrainian чепурити (čepurýty, “to dress up, to decorate”) (also чепурити (čepúryty)), чепуритися (čepurýtysja, “to be important”) (also чепуритися (čepúrytysja)), Belarusian чепурыцца (čjepurýcca, “to be important”), Slovene čepériti se (“to bristle one's feathers, to be important”) (tonal orthography), Greek τσοπορός (tsoporós, “cutesy”) (a borrowing from Slavic).
