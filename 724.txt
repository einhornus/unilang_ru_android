<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">reel</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">reels</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>A shaky or unsteady gait.
<ul><li><b>2010</b>, Andrew Koppelman, <i>The Gay Rights Question in Contemporary American Law</i> (page 92)

<li>A kind of spool, turning on an axis, on which yarn, threads, lines, or the like, are wound.
<dl><dd><i>a log <b>reel</b></i>, used by seamen</dd>

<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">textiles</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A machine on which yarn is wound and measured into lays and hanks, —-- for cotton or linen it is fifty-four inches in circuit; for worsted, thirty inches.
