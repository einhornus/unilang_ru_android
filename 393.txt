<dl><dd><i>Additional archaic forms are second-person singular present tense</i> <b>hast</b>, <i>third-person singular present tense</i> <b>hath</b>, <i>present participle</i> <b>haveing</b>, <i>and second-person singular past tense</i> <b>hadst</b>.</dd></dl>
<ol><li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">transitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To possess, own.
<dl><dd><i>I <b>have</b> a house and a car.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">transitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To hold, as something at someone's disposal.

<dd><i>Do you <b>have</b> the key?</i> (not necessarily one's own key)</dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">transitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> <span class="use-with-mention">Used to state the existence or presence of someone in a specified relationship with the subject</span>.
<dl><dd><i>I <b>have</b> two sisters.</i></dd>
<dd><i>She doesn't <b>have</b> any friends.</i></dd>
<dd><i>I <b>have</b> a really mean boss.</i></dd></dl></li>

<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">transitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To be scheduled to attend, undertake or participate in.
