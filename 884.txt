<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Numeral">Numeral</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">thousand</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn" lang="en">thousands</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>A numerical value equal to 1,000 = 10  |  100 = 10<sup>3</sup>
<dl><dd><i>The company earned fifty <b>thousand</b> dollars last month.</i></dd>
<dd><i>Many <b>thousands</b> of people came to the conference.</i></dd></dl></li></ol>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p>Unlike cardinal numerals such as <i>ten</i> or <i>ninety-nine</i> (where one can say e.g. <i>there were ten men present</i>), the word <i>thousand</i> is a noun like <i>dozen</i> and needs a determiner or another numeral to function as a numeral: one cannot say *<i>there were thousand men present</i>, but must say:
</p>
<ul><li><i>there were a <b>thousand</b> men / one <b>thousand</b> men / forty-three <b>thousand</b> men present</i></li>
<li>one can also speak of <i>the <b>thousand</b> men</i>, <i>several <b>thousand</b> men</i>, or <i>some <b>thousand</b> men who were present</i></li>
<li>compare <i>a dozen men / one dozen men / forty-three dozen men</i>, <i>the dozen men</i>, <i>several dozen men</i>, <i>some dozen men</i></li></ul>
<p>When preceded by a determiner or numeral and followed by <i>of</i>, it can be singular or plural:
</p>
<ul><li><i>two <b>thousand</b> of the inhabitants died</i>, <i>several <b>thousand</b> of the inhabitants fled</i></li>
<li><i>many <b>thousands</b> of women marched</i></li>
<li>"Aragorn should find some two <b>thousands</b> of those that he had gathered to him in the South; but Imrahil should find three and a half thousands; and Éomer five hundreds of the Rohirrim who were unhorsed but themselves warworthy." (J.R.R. Tolkien, <i>The Return of the King</i>)</li></ul>
<p>When followed by <i>of</i> and not preceded by a determiner or numeral, it must be pluralized with <i>-s</i>: <i><b>thousands</b> of women protested</i>, <i>countless <b>thousands</b> of women voted</i>, not *<i>thousand of women</i>.
</p>
