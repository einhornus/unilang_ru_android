дух (602)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *duxъ.

Noun[edit]

дух • (dux) | m | anim or m | inan (genitive ду́ха, nominative plural ду́хи, genitive plural ду́хов)

  1. spirit
    не в ду́хе ― ne v dúxe ― in a bad temper
  2. mind
  3. courage
  4. ghost
  5. breath
  6. scent
  7. rebel (especially in Afghanistan; from душман (dušman))
  8. (military, | slang) new recruit, rookie who is sometimes abused by the longer serving soldiers

Related terms[edit]

  • во́здух (vózdux)
  • ду́хи (duxí)
  • духо́вный (duxóvnyj)
  • духово́й (duxovój)
  • души́стый (dušístyj)
  • ду́шный (dúšnyj)
  • душо́к (dušók)
