север (1936)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *sěverъ, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱeh₁wero. Cognate with Lithuanian šiaurė, Latin caurus and English shower.

Noun[edit]

север • (séver) | m | inan (genitive се́вера, uncountable)

  1. north
  2. North (northern part of any region)
  3. Arctic (northern parts of Russia)
    Кра́йний Север ― Krájnij Séver ― Far North (Russian territories that lie to the north of the Arctic circle)

Synonyms[edit]

  • норд (nord) (nautical)
