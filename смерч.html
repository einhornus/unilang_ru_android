смерч (10435)


Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic смьрьчь (smĭrĭčĭ, “cloud”). Cognates include сморкать (smorkátʹ, “to blow one's nose”), морок (morok, “darkness, cloud, fog”) and мрак (mrak, “darkness”).

Noun[edit]

смерч • (smerč) | m | inan (genitive сме́рча, nominative plural сме́рчи, genitive plural сме́рчей)

  1. (meteorology) tornado, twister, whirlwind

Synonyms[edit]

  • торна́до | n (tornádo)
