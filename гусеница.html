гу́сеница (5589)


Etymology[edit]

Cognate with Old Church Slavonic юсеница (jusenica), Ukrainian гусениця (húsenycja), Bulgarian гъсеница (gǎsénica), Serbo-Croatian гу̏сеница/gȕsenica/гу̏сјеница/gȕsjenica, Slovene gosẹ̑nica, Czech housenka, Slovak husenica, Old Polish wąsienica, Polish gąsienica, Upper Sorbian husańca, Lower Sorbian guseńca. Probably from Proto-Slavic *ǫsěnica. The initial г (g)- is prothetic or by conflation with Common Slavonic гоущерица (gušterica, “lizard”) (Russian я́щерица (jáščerica)), and is not related to Russian гусь (gusʹ).

Noun[edit]

гу́сеница • (gúsenica) | f | anim or f | inan (genitive гу́сеницы, nominative plural гу́сеницы, genitive plural гу́сениц)

  1. caterpillar
  2. (mechanical) caterpillar track
