диспози́ция (24808)


Etymology[edit]

Ultimately borrowed from Latin dispositiō (“arrangement”).

Noun[edit]

диспози́ция • (dispozícija) | f | inan (genitive диспози́ции, nominative plural диспози́ции, genitive plural диспози́ций)

  1. (military) disposition (of troops, etc.)
  2. (historical, | military) written marching orders (in the Russian army during the 18th and 19th centuries)
  3. (psychology) disposition
