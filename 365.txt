<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">girlfriend</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">girlfriends</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>A female partner in an unmarried romantic relationship.
<dl><dd><span class="nyms coordinate-term"><span class="defdate">Coordinate terms:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">boyfriend</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">fiancée</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">lover</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">partner</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">significant other</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">sweetheart</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">wife</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">lady friend</span></span></dd>
<dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">Marc went to the park with his <b>girlfriend</b> and watched the sunset with her.</i></div></dd></dl>
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>1966</b>,  William Shatner as Capt. James T. Kirk,  “The Man Trap”, in  <cite>Star Trek: The Original Series</cite>, season 1, episode 1, Paramount Pictures:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">Want to pick some flowers, doctor? When a man visits an old <b>girlfriend</b> she usually expects something like that.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li>A female friend.
<dl><dd><span class="nyms synonym"><span class="defdate">Synonym:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">chickfriend</span> <span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content qualifier-content">slang</span><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">)</span></span></dd>
<dd><span class="nyms coordinate-term"><span class="defdate">Coordinate terms:</span> <span class="Latn" lang="en">buddy</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">friend</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">pal</span>, <span class="Latn" lang="en">BFF</span></span></dd>
<dd><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">Mary always enjoyed hanging out with her <b>girlfriend</b> Jessica.</i></div></dd></dl></li></ol>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<ul><li>The term is used to refer to any girl or woman in a romantic relationship with another person.</li>
<li>An adult woman in such a non-marital relationship is sometimes referred to instead as a <i><span class="Latn" lang="en">significant other</span></i> or <i><span class="Latn" lang="en">partner</span></i>, especially if the two partners are living together. Because <i>girlfriend</i> and <i>partner</i> mean different things to different people, the distinctions between the terms are subjective, and which term is used in a relationship will ultimately be determined by personal preference.</li>
<li>The use of <i>girlfriend</i> for a female non-romantic friend of another female is limited in geographic distribution and sometimes preferred by older generations of speakers.</li></ul>
