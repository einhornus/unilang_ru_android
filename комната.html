ко́мната (229)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic ко́мната (komnata), from Latin camīnāta, probably through Old High German [Term?] as evidenced by the initial stress. Cognate with English chimney.

Noun[edit]

ко́мната • (kómnata) | f | inan (genitive ко́мнаты, nominative plural ко́мнаты, genitive plural ко́мнат)

  1. room
