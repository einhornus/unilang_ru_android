шрам (5514)


Etymology[edit]

From Ukrainian шрам (šram), from Polish szram, szrama, from Middle High German schramme, schramm (German Schramme).

Noun[edit]

шрам • (šram) | m | inan (genitive шра́ма, nominative plural шра́мы, genitive plural шра́мов)

  1. scar, cicatrix (a permanent mark on the skin sometimes caused by the healing of a wound)

Synonyms[edit]

  • рубе́ц (rubéc), ра́на (rána)
