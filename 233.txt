<hr><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Verb">Verb</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">desert</strong> (<i>third-person singular simple present</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en 3 | s | pres-form-of" lang="en">deserts</b>, <i>present participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en pres | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">deserting</b>, <i>simple past and past participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en past | and | past | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">deserted</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>To leave (anything that depends on one's presence to survive, exist, or succeed), especially when contrary to a promise or obligation; to abandon; to forsake.
<dl><dd><i>You can't just drive off and <b>desert</b> me here, in the middle of nowhere.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li>To leave one's duty or post, especially to leave a military or naval unit without permission.
<dl><dd><i>Anyone found <b>deserting</b> will be punished.</i></dd></dl></li></ol>
