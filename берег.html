берег (406)


Etymology 1[edit]

From Old East Slavic берегъ (beregŭ), from Proto-Slavic *bergъ. Doublet of брег (breg), a borrowing from Old Church Slavonic.

Noun[edit]

берег • (béreg) | m | inan (genitive берега, nominative plural берега*, genitive plural берего́в) (* The irregular plural берега is actually an old dual form, since rivers have two of them.)

  1. bank, shore, coast, beach
  2. land
Usage notes[edit]
  • The stress may fall on the preposition in the expression на берег (ná bereg, “ashore”).
Synonyms[edit]
  • брег (breg) (poetic)
Related terms[edit]
  • прибре́жный (pribréžnyj)
  • прибрежье | n (pribréžʹje)
