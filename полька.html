по́лька (22022)


Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Polish Polka.

Noun[edit]

по́лька • (pólʹka) | f | anim (genitive по́льки, nominative plural по́льки, genitive plural по́лек, masculine поля́к)

  1. female Pole, Polish girl or woman
Synonyms[edit]
  • полячка (poljáčka) (colloquial)
  • ляшка (ljáška) (historical, can be slightly derogatory)
  • пшечка (pšéčka) (derogatory)

Related terms[edit]

  • Польша | f (Pólʹša)
  • по́льский (pólʹskij)

Borrowed from Czech polka (“dance in half”), from Czech polovina (“half”), from Proto-Slavic *polъ, further origin uncertain.

Noun[edit]

по́лька • (pólʹka) | f | inan (genitive по́льки, nominative plural по́льки, genitive plural по́лек)

  1. (music) polka
  2. polka (a haircut)
