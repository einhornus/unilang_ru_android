не́ту (1691)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic нѣту (nětu) and нѣтъ (nětŭ). From older *не ѥ ту́ (it is not here), where ту́ (тут, also colloquial нетуть (nétutĭ)), was originally an ethical dative (-ть (-tĭ), dative -ти (-ti)).

Usage notes[edit]

  • Often proscribed in favor of нет (net).
  • In the colloquial register, usage depends largely on the speaker's personal attitude towards this word; there is a range from active users to those who oppose it unconditionally.

Synonyms[edit]

  • нет (net)
