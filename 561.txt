<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">mouse</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">mice</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Any small rodent of the genus <i>Mus</i>.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>1892</b>,  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Besant" class="extiw" title="w:Walter Besant">Walter Besant</a>,  chapter II, in  <cite>The Ivory Gate: A Novel</cite>, New York, N.Y. |  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harper_(publisher)" class="extiw" title="w:Harper (publisher)">Harper amp; Brothers</a>,<span class="q-hellip-sp"> </span><span class="q-hellip-b">[</span><span title="Franklin Square">…</span><span class="q-hellip-b">]</span><span class="q-hellip-b"></span>, <small><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OCLC" class="extiw" title="w:OCLC">OCLC</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://worldcat.org/oclc/16832619">16832619</a></small>:</span><dl><dd></dd></dl></div>
<dl><dd>At twilight in the summer there is never anybody to fear—man, woman, or cat—in the chambers and at that hour the <b>mice</b> come out. They do not eat parchment or foolscap or red tape, but they eat the luncheon crumbs.</dd></dl></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">informal</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A member of the many small rodent and marsupial species resembling such a rodent.</li>
<li>A quiet or shy person.</li>
<li class="senseid" id="English-computing"> <span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">computing</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> (<i>plural</i> <b>mice</b> <i>or, rarely,</i> <b>mouses</b>) An input device that is moved over a pad or other flat surface to produce a corresponding movement of a pointer on a graphical display.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">boxing</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> Hematoma.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">nautical</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A turn or lashing of spun yarn or small stuff, or a metallic clasp or fastening, uniting the point and shank of a hook to prevent its unhooking or straightening out.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">obsolete</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> <span class="use-with-mention">A familiar term of endearment.</span>
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><i><abbr title="circa">c.</abbr></i> <b>1599–1602</b>,  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>,  <cite><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamlet" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hamlet">Hamlet</a></cite>, Act 3, Scene 4:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">Let the bloat King tempt you again to bed, / Pinch wanton on your cheek, call you his <b>mouse</b></span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li>A match used in firing guns or blasting.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">set theory</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A small model of (a fragment of) <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zermelo-Fraenkel_set_theory" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory">Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory</a> with desirable properties (depending on the context).</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">historical</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> A small cushion for a woman's hair.</li></ol>

<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">mouse</strong> (<i>third-person singular simple present</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en 3 | s | pres-form-of" lang="en">mouses</b>, <i>present participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en pres | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">mousing</b>, <i>simple past and past participle</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en past | and | past | ptcp-form-of" lang="en">moused</b>)
</p>
<ol><li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">intransitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To move cautiously or furtively, in the manner of a mouse (the rodent) (<i>frequently used in the phrasal verb</i> <b>to mouse around</b>).</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">intransitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To hunt or catch mice (the rodents), usually of cats.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">transitive<span class="ib-comma">,</span> nautical</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To close the mouth of a hook by a careful binding of marline or wire.
<dl><dd><i>Captain Higgins <b>moused</b> the hook with a bit of marline to prevent the block beckets from falling out under slack.</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">intransitive<span class="ib-comma">,</span> computing</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To navigate by means of a computer mouse.
<ul><li><b>1988</b>, <i>MacUser</i>, Volume 4
<dl><dd>I had just <b>moused</b> to the File menu and the pull-down menu repeated the menu <a href = vnd.unilang:бар.html>bar</a>'s hue a dozen shades lighter.</dd></dl></li>
<li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><b>2009</b>,  Daniel Tunkelang,  <cite>Faceted Search</cite>, page 35:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">Unlike the Flamenco work, the Relation Browser allows users to quickly explore a document space using dynamic queries issued by <b>mousing</b> over facet elements in the interface.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">obsolete<span class="ib-comma">,</span> nonce word<span class="ib-comma">,</span> transitive</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> To tear, as a cat devours a mouse.
<ul><li><div class="citation-whole"><span class="cited-source"><i><abbr title="circa">c.</abbr></i> <b>1596</b>,  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare" class="extiw" title="w:William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>,  “<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_John_(play)" class="extiw" title="w:King John (play)">The Life and Death of King Iohn</a>”, in  <cite>Mr. William Shakespeares Comedies, Histories, amp; Tragedies: Published According to the True Originall Copies</cite> (<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Folio" class="extiw" title="w:First Folio">First Folio</a>), London |  Printed by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Jaggard" class="extiw" title="w:William Jaggard">Isaac Iaggard</a>, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Blount" class="extiw" title="w:Edward Blount">Ed[ward] Blount</a>, published 1623, <small><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OCLC" class="extiw" title="w:OCLC">OCLC</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://worldcat.org/oclc/606515358">606515358</a></small>, [Act II, scene i]:</span><dl><dd><div class="h-quotation"><span class="Latn e-quotation" lang="en">[Death] <b>mousing</b> the flesh of men.</span></div></dd></dl></div></li></ul></li></ol>
