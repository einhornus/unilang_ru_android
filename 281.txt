<hr><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">elk</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en"><strong class="selflink">elk</strong></b> <i>or</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">elks</b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Any of various large species of deer such as red deer, moose or wapiti (see usage notes).
<ol><li>The subspecies of the moose (<span class="biota"><a href="https://species.wikimedia.org/wiki/Alces_alces_alces" class="extiw" title="wikispecies:Alces alces alces"><i>Alces alces alces</i></a></span>, alternatively named <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_elk" class="extiw" title="w:Eurasian elk">Eurasian elk</a> because the wapiti is also called elk) that occurs only in Europe and Asia (not to be confused with the species called moose {<i>Alces alces</i>} which encompasses all subspecies of the moose).</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">now chiefly <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe" class="extiw" title="w:Europe">Europe</a></span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The largest member of the deer family (<i>Alces alces</i>); a moose.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">Canada, US</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The common wapiti (<i>Cervus canadensis</i>); the second largest member of the deer family, smaller only than a moose.</li></ol></li></ol>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h5>
<p>Notice that the word elk is used in the same way as the word <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">caribou</i> is used for the subspecies of the <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">reindeer</i>. The only difference here is that it refers only to a single subspecies, while <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">caribou</i> refers to several subspecies of the <span class="Latn" lang="en">reindeer</span>.
</p>
