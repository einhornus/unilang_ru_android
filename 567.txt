Usage is not recommended where the accusative suffix is possible (on nouns and adjectives), but where it is not: numerals (<i class="Latn mention" lang="eo">unu</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">one</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>), particles (<i class="Latn mention" lang="eo">iom</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">some</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="eo">ties</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">that one's</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>), letters (<i>J</i>), titles of books, and quotations. More standard options are to use the general preposition <i class="Latn mention" lang="eo">je</i>, to omit the accusative ending, or to rephrase the sentence to avoid the issue.
</p>
