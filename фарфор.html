фарфо́р (13498)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ottoman Turkish فغفوری | (fağfûrî, “Chinese [porcelain]”), from Persian فغفوری | (fağfuri, “Chinese [porcelain]”), from فغفور | (fağfur, “emperor of China”) + ـی | (-i), from Sogdian [script needed] (βγpwr /βaγpūr/); see it for more.

Noun[edit]

фарфо́р • (farfór) | m | inan (genitive фарфо́ра, nominative plural фарфо́ры, genitive plural фарфо́ров)

  1. porcelain, china

Related terms[edit]

  • фарфо́ровый (farfórovyj)
