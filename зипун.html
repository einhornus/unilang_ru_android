зипу́н (29149)


Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Venetian zipón, possibly via Greek ζιπούνι (zipoúni), since the 16th century. Ultimately from Arabic جُبَّة | (jubba, “long garment”). Doublet of шу́ба (šúba), дже́мпер (džémper), ю́бка (júbka), and жупан (župán).

Noun[edit]

зипу́н • (zipún) | m | inan (genitive зипуна́, nominative plural зипуны́, genitive plural зипуно́в)

  1. (historical) zipun (medieval Russian type of peasant upper garment)
