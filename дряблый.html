дря́блый (16050)


Etymology[edit]

Compare дрябнуть (drjábnutʹ, “to become flabby”), дрябь | f (drjabʹ, “flabbiness”), from Proto-Slavic *dręb-.Per Vasmer, quoting Mikloshich, related to Lithuanian drìbti (“to trickle, to drip with thick drops”) (1sg. drimbù), drabnùs (“fat”), dramblỹs (“thick-chested; elephant”). Apparently also related to Ancient Greek θρόμβος (thrómbos, “clot”), θρομβεῖον (thrombeîon, “lump”).

Adjective[edit]

дря́блый • (drjáblyj) (comparative (по)дряблее or (по)дряблей)

  1. (colloquial) flabby, flaccid
  2. (figuratively) weak-willed, spineless

Synonyms[edit]

  • (flabby): вя́лый (vjályj)
  • (weak-willed): безво́льный (bezvólʹnyj), бесхарактерный (besxarákternyj)

Related terms[edit]

  • дрябь (drjabʹ)
