<hr><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h4>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">swan</strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">swans</b> <i>or</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en"><strong class="selflink">swan</strong></b>)
</p>
<ol><li>Any of various species of large, long-necked waterfowl, of genus <i>Cygnus</i> (bird family: Anatidae), most of which have white plumage.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">figuratively</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> One whose grace etc. suggests a swan.</li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">heraldry</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> This bird used as a heraldic charge, sometimes with a crown around its neck (e. g. the arms of Buckinghamshire).</li></ol>

<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_2">Etymology 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p>Probably from dialectal <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">I s’wan</i>, a corruption of <i>I shall warrant</i>; or possibly from a minced form of <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">I swear on</i>.
