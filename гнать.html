гнать (2133)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *gъnati, from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰénti, from *gʷʰen-, whence English fend; defend; offend.

Verb[edit]

гнать • (gnatʹ) | impf (perfective погна́ть or загна́ть)

  1. to drive
  2. to distill
  3. to chase, to pursue
  4. to speed along
  5. (slang) to lie
  6. (slang) to give (used in the imperative to make a forceful demand)

Usage notes[edit]

  • Гнать is in the class of Russian concrete verbs. Its counterpart, гоня́ть (gonjátʹ), is in the class of Russian abstract verbs.
