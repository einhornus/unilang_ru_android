ту́та (17850)


Etymology 1

Borrowed from Ottoman Turkish طوت | (tut, dut), from Middle Persian [script needed] (tūt), possibly from Aramaic ܬܘܬܐ | /תותא | (tuta), or possibly the Aramaic was derived from the Persian.

Noun

ту́та • (túta) f inan (genitive ту́ты, nominative plural ту́ты, genitive plural тут)

  1. mulberry (Morus)
Synonyms
  • тут (tut)
  • тутовник (tutovnik)
  • тутовое де́рево (tutovoje dérevo)
  • шелкови́ца (šelkovica)
Related terms
  • тутовый (tutovyj)

Etymology 2

Adverb

ту́та • (túta)

  1. (colloquial, nonstandard) Alternative form of тут (tut, “here”)
Synonyms
  • здесь (zdesʹ)
  • тут (tut)

Etymology 3

Noun

ту́та • (túta) m inan

  1. genitive singular of тут (tut)
