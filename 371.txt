<p>From <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English" class="extiw" title="w:Middle English">Middle English</a></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="enm">good</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English" class="extiw" title="w:Old English">Old English</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="ang">gōd</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Germanic_language" class="extiw" title="w:Proto-Germanic language">Proto-Germanic</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="gem-pro">*gōdaz</i>, from <span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language" class="extiw" title="w:Proto-Indo-European language">Proto-Indo-European</a></span> <i class="Latinx mention" lang="ine-pro">*gʰedʰ-</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">to unite, be associated, suit</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>. <h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h5>
<p>The comparative <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">gooder</i> and superlative <i class="Latn mention" lang="en">goodest</i> are nonstandard.
In informal (often jocular) contexts, <i>best</i> may be inflected further and given the comparative <i>bester</i> and the superlative <i>bestest</i>; these forms are also nonstandard.
