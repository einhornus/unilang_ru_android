семья́ (420)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic сѣмиꙗ (sěmija, “family, household; husband, wife”) (whence also Ukrainian сім'я (simʺjá) and Belarusian сям'я (sjamʺjá)), from Proto-Slavic *sěmьja. Compare Lithuanian šeimà (“family”), Latvian sàimе (“family, household”), Old Prussian seimīns, Gothic 𐌷𐌰𐌹𐌼𐍃 (haims, “settlement”), Old High German heim (“home”) and thereby English home, Ancient Greek κώμη (kṓmē, “settlement”).

Noun[edit]

семья́ • (semʹjá) | f | inan (genitive семьи, nominative plural семьи, genitive plural семе́й)
