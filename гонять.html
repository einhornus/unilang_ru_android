гоня́ть (4540)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *gaňati, from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰen- (“to kill”). Cognate with Albanian gjan (“I hunt”), Lithuanian giñti, Old Norse gunnr (“fight”), Latin de-fendo, Ancient Greek θείνω (theínō), Armenian գան (gan, “beating, blows”).

Verb

гоня́ть • (gonjátʹ) impf (perfective погоня́ть)

  1. to drive
  2. to distill
  3. to chase, to pursue
  4. to speed alonǵ

Usage notes

Гоня́ть and погоня́ть (pogonjátʹ) are in the class of Russian abstract verbs. Its counterparts, гнать (gnatʹ) and погна́ть (pognátʹ), are in the class of Russian concrete verbs.

  7. жарг. встреча́ться с кем-либо, состоя́ть в отноше́ниях | Они гоня́ют уже́ два ме́сяца.
