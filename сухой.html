сухо́й (963)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *suxъ. Cognates include German sohr, Ancient Greek αὖος (aûos), Latvian sauss, Lithuanian saũsas, Persian خشک‎ | (xošk), Sanskrit शुष्क (śuṣka).

Adjective[edit]

сухо́й • (suxój) (comparative (по)су́ше, superlative са́мый сухо́й)

  1. dry
  2. lean
  3. (relational) land
  4. (figuratively) cool, cold
  5. boring, dull
